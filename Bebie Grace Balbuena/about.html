Nai Ebing's Sweet Corner

"Made with love and sweetness"

When you eat rice cake with your family and friends, it always tastes better!

Rice cake is a type of baked rice cake from the Philippines. It is usually eaten for
breakfast,especially during the Christmas season. It is traditionally cooked in clay pots lined
with leaves. It is a subtype of kakanin (rice cakes) in Philippine cuisine. Bibingka is also
found in Christian communities in eastern Indonesia.


NESC (Nai Ebing Sweet Corner) business started in 1957. Rice cake is their family
delicacy. Our ‘grandlola’ Lamberta Saranillo started the food business, when she got
married,she managed it herself and with the help of our great² lolo she made her business grow and she got a lot of satisfied customers.

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, people have been hustling for work, finding no time to invest in their wellbeing. Last May 2021 we decided to start the business which is called NESC (Nai Ebing’s Sweet Corner), and because of its unique and delicious taste Nai Ebing’s Sweet Corner got lots of customers until this rice cake business became known not just in southern Cebu but moreover in Cebu City we've got a seller there which is a journalist in Brigada News.


NESC (Nai Ebing’s Sweet Corner) was named after Eduvigis Taypin who was the second daughter of Lamberta Saranillo. When Lamberta died, Eduvigis Taypin continued the rice cake business. NESC busines is located at 'barangay' Bugas, Badian, Cebu and now they are currently selling delicious rice cakes and creating different new recipes.


And now they are looking forward to more great opportunities and small successes in their business.
